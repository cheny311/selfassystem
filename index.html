The Story of A Chopstick

A Chopstick

Chopsticks, the essential household eating utensils that people use in different places use these tools, especially in China, people can find them everywhere. They come in different shapes, sizes, represent different cultures and wishes. They always expected to come in pairs, well-conditioned, and good-looking to make themselves more attractive to people.

I see myself in that. Growing up, I faced many expectations.

There is always the desire that I want to break from these, for example, how society taught women to behave in a certain way, along with how my parents told me to be a certain kind of person. There are some feelings that I feel embarrassed to speak about and I often use other objects to express my feelings. In symbolizing my silent self, the chopstick contains identities and longings too delicate, yet meaningful, to expose.
